🏆 2nd Runner-Up — BRACU EEE FYDP Project Showcase 2026

Soft Robotic Manipulation System
4-DoF Manipulator + Bio-Inspired Soft Gripper

94.7% YOLOv11-m Precision
0.86 mAP@0.50:0.95
0.77 m³ Reachable Volume
98.99% Sim Positioning Accuracy
4.06s Avg Sim Execution Time
4 Models CV Models Evaluated

A soft robotic manipulation system integrating a 4-DoF rigid manipulator with a bio-inspired soft gripper for adaptive grasping of delicate and irregularly shaped objects. Supports both manual teleoperation and autonomous control via a ROS2 + MoveIt2 architecture with collision-aware motion planning. Vision pipeline benchmarks four deep-learning models on an 11-class manipulation dataset — YOLOv11-m achieves the best balance of accuracy and speed (94.7% precision, 94.3% recall). Fabricated via 3D printing from Fusion 360 designs; kinematics validated in simulation and real-world experiments.

Safe manipulation of delicate and irregularly shaped objects remains a major challenge for conventional rigid robotic systems. This project presents a structured, model-based design approach beginning with theoretical kinematic and dynamic analysis and payload torque calculations to guide actuator selection. The manipulator and gripper are evaluated in Autodesk Fusion 360 including static stress and motion studies, then fabricated using 3D printing. A URDF-based robot description in ROS2/MoveIt2 enables collision-aware motion planning, workspace analysis, and repeatable end-effector positioning. Numerical workspace sampling confirms an asymmetric reachable volume of approximately 0.77 m³ under joint and self-collision constraints. Four deep learning models — YOLOv11-m, Faster R-CNN, RF-DETR, and RTMDet-m — are trained on an 11-class dataset; YOLOv11-m achieves 94.7% precision, 94.3% recall, and mAP@0.50:0.95 of 0.86. In simulation, autonomous control achieves 98.99% mean positioning accuracy (avg 4.06s) vs 70.08% for manual (avg 46.72s). Physical experiments on fragile objects show 33% grasp success in fully autonomous mode (avg 23s) and 66% positioning accuracy under manual teleoperation (avg 51s). Results confirm feasibility while highlighting hardware/actuation limitations and clear paths for improvement in industrial automation, agriculture, and service robotics.
